The nitrogen cycle moves nitrogen between the air, the soil, and living things, and it is essential because every protein and strand of DNA depends on it. Although the atmosphere is almost four fifths nitrogen gas, most organisms cannot use that gas directly. Special bacteria in the soil and in the roots of certain plants perform nitrogen fixation, turning the inert gas into compounds that roots can absorb. Other bacteria later break those compounds back down and return free nitrogen gas to the air. Farmers lean heavily on this cycle, and on manufactured fertiliser, to keep their soil rich enough to grow crops.
