Plate tectonics is the theory that the outer shell of the Earth is broken into rigid plates that drift slowly over a hot, soft layer below. Where two plates pull apart, molten rock rises to form new ocean floor along the mid ocean ridges. Where they collide, one plate can dive beneath the other and melt, feeding chains of volcanoes and lifting jagged mountain ranges. Most large earthquakes happen along the grinding boundaries where two plates lock together and then suddenly slip. The plates move about as fast as a fingernail grows, yet over millions of years they rearrange whole continents.
