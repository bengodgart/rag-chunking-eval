Cellular respiration is the process that living cells use to release the energy stored in glucose. Oxygen drawn in from the air reacts with sugar to produce carbon dioxide, water, and a usable energy currency the cell can spend. Most of that energy is captured as molecules of adenosine triphosphate, the rechargeable battery that powers almost every task inside the cell. Respiration is in many ways the mirror image of photosynthesis, running the same atoms in the opposite direction. It happens continuously, day and night, in the cells of plants and animals alike.
