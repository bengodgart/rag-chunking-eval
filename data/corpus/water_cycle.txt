The water cycle is the constant journey water makes between the ocean, the atmosphere, and the land. Heat from the sun drives evaporation, lifting water vapour off the ocean surface and into the air where it cools and condenses into clouds. When the droplets grow heavy enough they fall back as rain or snow in the step known as precipitation. Some of that water soaks into the ground to refill hidden aquifers while the rest runs downhill through rivers back to the sea. The whole cycle carries no new water, it simply moves the same ancient supply around again and again.
