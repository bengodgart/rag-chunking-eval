Photosynthesis is the process green plants use to turn sunlight into chemical energy that the cell can store and spend later. Inside each leaf, tiny structures called chloroplasts capture light and combine carbon dioxide from the air with water pulled up through the roots. The reaction releases oxygen into the atmosphere as a by product and locks energy away inside molecules of glucose. Most of the free oxygen on Earth was produced by photosynthesis over billions of years of slow accumulation. Without this single process the food chains that feed almost every living thing on the planet would quietly collapse.
