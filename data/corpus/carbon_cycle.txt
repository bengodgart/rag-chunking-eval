The carbon cycle describes how the element carbon moves between the air, the ocean, living things, and the rocks beneath our feet. Plants pull carbon dioxide out of the atmosphere during photosynthesis and animals return it through respiration and decay. Over far longer spans, carbon is buried in ocean sediment and slowly pressed into limestone and fossil fuels underground. Burning those fuels releases carbon that was locked away for millions of years back into the air in a geological instant. This sudden release is the main reason the atmosphere is warming faster than it has in the deep past.
