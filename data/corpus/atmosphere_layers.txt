The atmosphere is wrapped around the Earth in layers, each with its own character. The lowest layer, the troposphere, is where nearly all weather happens and where the air we breathe is found. Above it the stratosphere holds the ozone layer, a thin shield of gas that soaks up harmful ultraviolet light from the sun. Higher still, the air grows so thin that meteors burn up and the sky fades into the black of space. Temperature does not fall steadily with height, it rises and falls from one layer to the next, which is exactly how scientists tell the layers apart.
